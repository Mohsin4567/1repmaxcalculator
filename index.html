Using This Tool

Get a real number

01

Pick a true working set

Use a set taken close to failure — one where the last rep or two was genuinely hard. A set with reps left in the tank underestimates your max.

02

Stay under 10 reps

Every formula here is built on lower-rep data. Above ~12 reps, fatigue and technique breakdown skew the estimate lower than reality.

03

Re-test every few weeks

An estimate isn't a substitute for a real max attempt, but it's a low-risk way to track whether your training is actually working.
